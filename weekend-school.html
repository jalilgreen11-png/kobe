Curriculum — 1447 / 1448

What we teach

Four periods across Saturdays and Sundays. Two classes Saturday with a thirty-minute break. Three classes Sunday with ten-minute breaks between.

Saturday · Period 1

How to Read the Qur'an / Qur'an Memorization

We first teach the student how to read the Arabic language, then how to read from the mushaf, then for him or her to memorize what he or she can confidently read. Source: Help Yourself in Reading Qur'an.

Saturday · Period 2

Arabic Language Basics

A basic understanding of how the Arabic language works to better understand the Qur'an, the Sunnah, and the scholars of Ahlus Sunnah wal-Jama'ah. Sources: Madinah Arabic Course, Essentials of Qur'anic Arabic, and Clear Arabic Grammar (an-Nahw al-Waadih).

Saturday · Period 3

As-Salaat (The Prayer)

To learn how to pray as the Prophet ﷺ prayed, based on evidence — including purification, wudu, ghusl, tayammum, and all prerequisites of the prayer. Sources: The Prophet's Prayer Described by al-Albani and other classical descriptions.

Sunday · Period 1

Tawheed & Aqeedah

For the student to articulate what a Muslim is to correctly believe in, based on evidence from the Qur'an and the Sunnah. Source: One Hundred Questions and Answers Regarding Tawheed and Aqeedah.

Sunday · Period 2

The Forty Hadith of Imam an-Nawawi

Memorization of the forty foundational ahadith of Imam an-Nawawi along with a basic understanding of their meanings and application in daily life.
